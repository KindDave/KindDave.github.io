Full Bio

Idowu David Awoyemi (preferred: David) is a doctoral candidate (ABD) in Instructional Technology in the Department of Educational Leadership, Policy, and Technology Studies at the University of Alabama. His research focuses on the design and application of emerging technologies — particularly extended reality (XR), immersive environments, and artificial intelligence (AI) — to develop adaptive and effective learning solutions for complex educational and training contexts.

His scholarly interests include AI-powered adaptive learning environments, the pedagogical integration of XR technologies, learning analytics, and innovative approaches to instructional design. He serves as Lab Manager for the Adaptive Design of Immersive e-Learning (ADIE) Lab and has collaborated with faculty on numerous research projects and conference presentations, earning recognition including the Most Outstanding Graduate Student in Research Award (2025), the Alabama Power Innovation and Technology Award (2025), and a Google PhD Fellowship University Nomination (2025).

His dissertation, titled "Development of Evidence-Based Immersive Virtual Reality-Supported Construction Safety Engineering Training," is supervised by Dr. Jewoong Moon (Chair). The three-article dissertation addresses construction safety education through: (1) a Design and Development Research study creating and usability-testing an IVR prototype on Horizon Worlds using Meta Quest headsets; (2) a microgenetic behavioral analysis examining moment-to-moment student learning processes using think-aloud protocols and sequential pattern mining; and (3) development and validation of an AI-powered formative feedback (AFF) framework using a Convolutional Neural Network that processes voice inputs, motion data, and interaction logs to automatically generate post-session diagnostic feedback. He was recognized as an Outstanding Student during both his undergraduate and graduate studies in Nigeria, and has been featured in Vanguard Nigeria for his work revolutionizing civil engineering education with VR technology.
